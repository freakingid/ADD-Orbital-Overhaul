## 7. Version History

- **v1.0** — Initial build: ship/asteroids/saucers, shield, killer satellite + homing wedges, waves, synth audio.
- **v1.1** — Radioactive salvage: garbage drops with decay, verlet tow chain (mass/momentum penalties, momentum tug), chain vulnerability with aft-severing, relocating recycling dock with escalating delivery scores, cargo HUD + dock pointer, pickup/deliver SFX. New constants block; `shortDelta` helper; `Garbage`/`FloatText`/`Dock` classes; chain physics functions after `shieldDeflect`. Headless-tested: pickup, tow, wrap, delivery scoring, severing.
- **v1.2** — *v2.0 Phase 1 (F1): Larger scrolling world & camera.* Simulation space split from the screen: a 3840×2160 toroidal world (`WORLD_W/H`) with a 1280×720 viewport (`VIEW_W/H`) that scrolls to keep the ship centered (`game.camera`). All wrap-aware helpers (`wrap`/`dist2`/`angleTo`/`shortDelta`/`wrapNode`) retargeted to `WORLD_*`; added `wrapOffset`/`wrapPos`. `draw()` gained a camera transform, wrap-aware per-entity nearest-image rendering + viewport culling (`onScreen`/`drawEntity`), and a world-spanning seamless starfield; HUD/overlays drawn screen-fixed after restore. Spawns (wave asteroids, dock) now ship-relative rings; satellites/saucers enter from viewport edges relative to the ship (feel unchanged). New constants: `CULL_MARGIN`, `SPAWN_MIN/MAX_DIST`, `DOCK_MIN/MAX_DIST`, `STAR_DENSITY`. Headless-tested: camera-follow, world-boundary wrap (x & y), no wrap at old 1280 boundary, ship-relative spawn reachability across the seam, draw() crash-free in all states.
- **v1.3** — *v2.0 Phase 2 (F2): Health Points & Knockback.* Discrete lives replaced by a single HP pool (`SHIP_MAX_HP` = 250) for the whole run; all respawn/lives logic removed (`game.lives`/`nextExtraLife`/`respawnTimer` gone, plus the "respawn only when center is clear" gate). New `damageShip()` handles unshielded hits: source-specific damage (`DMG_SMALL/MEDIUM/LARGE` 20/35/50, `DMG_BULLET` 15, via each hazard's new `damage` field), a `KNOCKBACK_SPEED` (250 px/s) shove away from the hazard, and a `HIT_STUN_DURATION` (1.0 s) i-frame window (reusing `ship.invuln`). `killShip()` is now a respawn-free game over at 0 HP. `addScore()` milestone changed from extra-life to a +25 HP hull repair (`REPAIR_*`), converting to a flat score bonus at full HP. HUD: HULL/HP bar (`COLOR.hp`) replaces the life-ship icons, styled like the shield bar. New `AudioSys.hit()` damage thud. Headless-tested (`scratchpad/test-f2.js`, 54 assertions): per-hit-type damage, hit-stun double-hit prevention, knockback direction/magnitude, 0-HP game over with no respawn, milestone repair, and shield still blocking damage+knockback.
- **v1.4** — *v2.0 Phase 3 (F3 Debris Satellites + F5 variable-mass garbage).* The `Asteroid` class → **`DebrisSatellite`** (array `game.asteroids` → `game.debris`; `AST_*` constants → `DEBRIS_*`; `destroyAsteroid` → `destroyDebris`; `COLOR.asteroid` → `COLOR.debris`); redrawn as a broken-satellite silhouette (irregular `hull` polygon + 1–2 antenna/panel `shards`). Splits changed to 3-way at large/medium (small destroyed), and **every** tier's destruction emits exactly `DEBRIS_GARBAGE` (3) canisters — replacing the probabilistic `GARBAGE_DROP` — so a fully-cleared large lineage yields 39 canisters. F5: `Garbage` and chain nodes gained a **`mass`** field (default 1.0); `Garbage.fromNode()` carries it, pickup copies it onto the node, and the chain thrust/top-speed penalties + momentum-tug `massFactor` now scale off **`chainMass()`** (Σ node.mass) instead of `chain.length` — identical feel for all-mass-1.0 cargo, the groundwork for F4's low-mass Hunter scrap. First-pass balance for the ~8× garbage volume: `GARBAGE_DECAY` 20→12, new `GARBAGE_SEVER_DECAY` 10 (was inline 15), wave spawn `min(4+w,11)`→`min(3+w,9)`. Headless-tested (`scratchpad/test-f3.js`, 28 assertions): 39-canister lineage count, per-tier splits, mass propagation (default/fromNode/pickup/sever), and the mass-sum equivalence of an 8× mass-1.0 chain vs a 16× mass-0.5 chain across thrust, top speed, and tug.
- **v1.5** — *v2.0 Phase 4 (F10 difficulty ramp + saucer calming).* A global difficulty ramp: `difficultyFactor(wave)` = `1 − e^(−(wave−1)/RAMP_WAVES)` (0 at wave 1, → 1 over many waves; `RAMP_WAVES` = 8 is the single ramp knob) and a `ramp(floor, ceil, wave)` interpolation helper, both in the Helpers block. The **saucers** are re-expressed as floor/ceiling pairs scaled through `ramp` (§2.6, §2.13): spawn gap `rand(20,30)s` → `rand(12,16)s` (re-expressing the old `rand(12,22)−min(6,wave)`); a fire-rate multiplier `1.8×` → `1.0×` applied via a new `Saucer.rollFireTimer(range)` to both saucers' reloads and first-shot delay; small-saucer aim error `±0.35` → `±0.09` rad; and small-saucer appearance chance `15%` → `60%`, which **folds in** (replaces) the old score>8000 gate so escalation is wave-driven in one place. Pillar 6 ("Ease players in") flipped from planned to shipped. No behavior/shape changes; the Hunter Satellite (§2.5) and Debris-density scaling are deferred to their own phases. Headless-tested (`scratchpad/test-f4.js`, 30 assertions): the `difficultyFactor` curve shape across waves 1–25, and each saucer parameter (gap/fire/aim/chance) at wave 1 vs wave 20 in the right direction and magnitude — including end-to-end through a real `Saucer`'s fired-bullet aim error and `rollFireTimer`.
- **v1.6** — *v2.0 Phase 5 (F4 Hunter Satellites).* The killer `Satellite` + homing `Wedge` classes were merged into one **`HunterSatellite`** class — a teal diamond family with three tiers (3 large / 2 medium / 1 small). Garbage at every tier: 3 normal-mass (1.0) at large/medium; a burst of `HUNTER_SMALL_GARBAGE` (6) **low-mass** (0.5) canisters at small, rendered in a paler tint (`COLOR.garbageLight`) as an easy-to-tow bonus. Contact damage 60/45/30 by tier (`HUNTER_DAMAGE`, above the equivalent Debris tier). Shield: the core bounces, homers are destroyed on contact and still split; Hunters still sever the tow chain. Headless-tested (`scratchpad/test-f5.js`, 40 assertions): full-lineage 13 kills / 66-canister tiered-mass count, per-tier splits & garbage mass, damage/score tables, the difficulty ramp on every tier (wave-1 floor vs wave-20), active-homing re-aim, and chain-severing on contact; f2/f3/f4 regressions updated to the merged entity and re-run green.

- **v3.2 (shipped)** — specs in `PLANNED-FEATURES-v3.2.md`, build order in `IMPLEMENTATION-PHASES-v3.2.md`. **P1: garbage clumps become physical, and un-hookable** — reverses the v3.1 FORK-2 decision (which was only correct while clumps were hookable; P2 makes them un-hookable, removing FORK-2's reason). A `Garbage` clump's **`mass` now sums** on merge (a wad of 4 normal canisters is mass 4.0) and its **`radius` derives** as `7·√pieces` (a single piece still reads 7; an 11-clump ~23 px). The merge velocity switched from the literal vector sum to the **momentum sum** `(mₐvₐ + m_b v_b)/(mₐ+m_b)` (pre-merge masses) — with mass summing and garbage soon permanent, a literal sum made clumps the fastest things on screen; now a heavy clump barely speeds up eating a fast light piece. Attraction became **mass-weighted** and gravity-like (each piece accelerates toward the other ∝ the other's mass), momentum-conserving and reducing *exactly* to the shipped symmetric pull at mass 1.0 (so `GARBAGE_MAGNET_PULL` is unretuned) — heavy clumps become slow anchors loose singles fall into. `Garbage.draw()` renders a clump as a **cluster** of `pieces` mini-canisters at golden-angle offsets (`θ=k·2.39996`, `r=5·√k`) tumbling on `this.spin`; `pieces===1` is byte-identical to before. **A clump (`pieces>1`) cannot be hooked** (the pickup gate now requires `pieces===1`) and the **Magnet ignores it** — you must shoot it apart first (the shatter tool is P2, below). Clumps stay **non-solid** to the ship (FORK-A2: zero contact damage, not in `hazards`). No new constant (7 is the existing canister radius); decay/bullet-collision/emission untouched (later phases). Headless-tested (`scratchpad/test-v31-coalesce.js` extended, **48 assertions** — the old exact-vector-sum assertion rewritten to the momentum sum): mass sums across a chain of merges, radius tracks `7·√pieces`, momentum-sum merge (a heavy clump absorbing a fast light piece barely speeds up), the mass-1.0 attraction reduction is bit-exact to the shipped force, a `pieces>1` clump is NOT hooked and the Magnet won't pull it while a single still hooks + is pulled, and `draw()` is crash-free at `pieces=1` and `pieces=11`. Full regression (all 18 test files, **834 assertions**) re-run green (`test-p5`/`test-p6` canister stubs gained `pieces:1` to match the new hook contract). See §2.5.1/§2.10. **P2: shatter a clump with one shot** — the tool that reopens what P1 made un-hookable. A new player-bullet-vs-garbage collision branch (in the non-hostile bullet loop, alongside the existing debris/saucer/hunter branches) targets ONLY `pieces > 1` clumps (tested against the P1-derived `radius`); a `pieces === 1` single is still not a bullet target and player bullets still pass straight through it, and hostile (saucer) bullets still pass through all garbage regardless of `pieces`. A hit calls the new flow function **`shatterClump(g)`** (shaped like `destroyDebris`/`destroyHunter`): `g.dead = true`, a small `boom()`, and exactly `g.pieces` fresh `new Garbage` singles pushed at `(g.x, g.y)`, each at `mass = g.mass / g.pieces` (per-piece mass identity is averaged back out — FLAG A-3, an accepted fidelity trade), `pieces = 1`, a **full, re-armed `coalesceDelay`** (the constructor default — reuses the P3-era constant, not a second one) so the burst disperses before it can re-clump, and an outward kick (new constant **`GARBAGE_SHATTER_KICK`**, `rand(40,90)` px/s) added to a fraction of the clump's own drift, mirroring the `destroyDebris` fan-out pattern. **No score** — the reward is the newly-hookable canisters. Decay, emission counts, the coalescence pass itself, and the Hunter transform are all untouched (P3 owns those). Headless-tested (`scratchpad/test-v31-coalesce.js` extended, **61 assertions**, +13 over P1): one player bullet on a `pieces=7` clump yields exactly 7 live singles, all `pieces===1`, all with a full `coalesceDelay`, all `mass = clumpMass/7` (total mass conserved), and they do NOT re-merge on the very next frame (the delay gate holds, driven through the real `coalesceGarbage`/`update`); a player bullet passes THROUGH a `pieces=1` canister untouched; a hostile bullet passes through a clump untouched; the emitted singles ARE hookable (the P1 gate now lets them through) via two real `update()` frames; shattering leaves the Waste-Not stat unchanged. The lab's text→ARPAbet layer (`WORDS` dictionary + rule guesser + `g2p`) is a *composition tool* whose output — each line's `phon` string — is already baked into `VOICE_LINES`, so it is dead code in the game and stays in the lab (which is where a new line's `phon` is derived, then its `{text,phon}` pasted in). This matches the `tools/` philosophy: port the *result*, not the instrument. **(1) `VoiceSys` — a SEPARATE module alongside AudioSys/MusicSys**, never folded into AudioSys (a flat bag of one-shots that must not grow a sequencer — MusicSys is the exact precedent). Owns its radio-character processing chain (highpass/lowpass/tanh-shaper/compressor + a squelch-gated static bed, default-on radio character) feeding **`AudioSys.voice`**. Every entry point is `if (!AudioSys.ctx) return;`-guarded — a total no-op headless (what makes it headless-safe). **(2) THE VOICE BUS IS FOUR SITES, not the one the spec claimed** — all verified: (a) `AudioSys.vol` gained `voice:1`; (b) `init()` builds `this.voice` → master mirroring sfx/music; (c) `setVol`'s node picker is now a `this[cat]` **lookup** — the old `master?…:sfx?…:music` ternary would have silently written `setVol("voice",…)` to the *music* gain; (d) `loadSettings`'s volume loop now derives its category list from `Object.keys(AudioSys.vol)` so a new bus persists without a fifth edit. A **Voice Volume** row was added to P4's Sound/Music screen by growing `SOUND_ROWS`/`VOL_LABELS`/`VOL_CATS` together (`voice`), persisted **additively** into the frozen `afd_settings_v1` (no schema bump); the `menuSound`/`drawSound` handlers are label-driven, so the row was purely additive (panel grown 460→510 px). **(3) LINES ARE DATA:** `VOICE_LINES` is keyed by event, each event an **array** of `{text,phon}` alternatives — adding a line is a one-line data edit. Selection is a **plain random pick** (§11d). **(4) Cooldown / priority (§11e), designed not hacked:** `VOICE_COOLDOWN` (1.2 s) — no line starts while one plays or within that of the last one ending; `VOICE_PRIORITY` (default 1; 3 = hull critical, 2 = health milestones) lets a higher line **pre-empt** a lower one that's playing, an equal/lower one is **dropped**. **Superseded lines DROP — never queue.** **A real bug the test caught:** the "nothing has played" sentinel had to be `busyUntil = -Infinity`, not 0 — with 0, every line in the AudioContext's first 1.2 s (`now < 0 + VOICE_COOLDOWN`) would have been swallowed by a phantom cooldown. The burst case (`hit → low HP → grab a powerup` in one instant) is tested: hull-critical takes the channel, the powerup grab is dropped. **(5) Triggers + latches — every anchor verified against the live code, not the spec:** **hull-low** rides `update()`'s existing rising edge but gets its **own** latch `game.lowHpVoiced` that `openPause`/`quitToTitle` do NOT tear down (they reset the *siren* latch, which is why the siren rising edge legitimately re-fires on unpause — the voice latch is the fix for Dan re-announcing "Hull integrity is critical." on every unpause; cleared only on the genuine falling edge + `startGame`); **hull-relief** on the falling edge, **additive** to P3's `hpReliefFlash` exhale (not a replacement, and not gated on voice availability — parity, four onset tells vs. two relief tells); **hull-full** a new edge latch `game.hpFullVoice` on `hp >= SHIP_MAX_HP` (the spec's claimed "full-HP special-case seam" in `applyPowerup` **does not exist** — a bare `hp = min(max, hp+amt)` — so the latch lives in `update()`, catching healing from the Health powerup AND `addScore`'s repair milestone for free; starts **true** at spawn so a fresh full-HP run doesn't blurt "brand new ship"); **collected** in `applyPowerup` (scoop in its early-return branch; Health silent — it speaks via the hull-full latch; a same-type bank re-pickup re-fires and the §11e cooldown drops the second line if it lands in-window — a decided, acceptable behavior); **expired** routed through **`powerActive(type)`** per timed type, never `powerFx` (so it catches count modes — the assertion the test proves with a pieces-mode Magnet whose `powerFx` is always 0), stored in `game.powerVoiced{}`; **scoop-lost** at the damage-decay site in `damageShip`; **dock delivery** (FORK-5 = b) on the pop that **empties** the chain (5–9/10–14/15–19/20+ tiers by `game.deliveryCount`; 1–4 silent) — the offload block runs only while `chain.length > 0`, so the emptying pop happens once and **the gate is the latch**, no flag; `breakChain`/`scatterChain` also zero `deliveryCount` but never reach the site, so they stay silent (asserted); **truck-full** on the pickup where `chain.length` reaches the **runtime** `game.cargoMax` (12→24), placed after the single/clump branches so both trip it, again gate-as-latch, re-arming after a delivery (a clump-scoop that fills the chain fires exactly once — asserted). `startGame` resets the three latches + calls `VoiceSys.reset()`.
